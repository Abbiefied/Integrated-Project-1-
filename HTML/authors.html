Ganiyat Abiola Adekunle

Ganiyat Adekunle is a young focused leader from Ibadan, Nigeria, who consciously and unconsciously inspires people around her. She is currently pursuing a degree in Computing at the African Leadership University (Mauritius). Prior to that, she had a diploma in Science Laboratory Technology and has experiences in many fields such as medical, business and technology. Ganiyat, through her critical thinking, sees a future where the education system is improved through the use of technology allowing students to relate the knowledge they acquire within the four walls of the school to solve real-life problems.

She is always charged towards taking up challenges and overcoming adversities. Ganiyat is also a proactive and strong advocate of SDG 4 (Quality Education), SDG 9(Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure), and SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities). She believes every child deserves a quality education and the world needs more critical thinkers and problem-solvers than ordinary earners.

She took an interest in Science and Technology because people in that field are considered smart. As a fashion designer with interest in software development and AI, her goal is to make wearable technologies that are actually useful and not some expensive ineffectual items like the common smart watches.
As the facilitator of the project, she designed the homepage, navbar, footer and was in charge of scheduling and moderating meetings. She resolved conflicts in members' codes and made sure everyone is on track. She worked on the website integrations (chatbot and disqus). She gave constructive feedbacks where needed.

Funke Esther

Funke Aderoju is a leader, entrepreneur, and activist, she hails from Osun state, Nigeria but she lived in Lagos all her life.

She plans to make an impact through technology by creating a mental help website where people facing mental health problems can talk to an online therapist for free, this can also help to prevert the number of suicide in the country.

She contributed immensely to the success of the website by creating the ALU page, she also reminded team members to attend meetings on time, made observations about each page and where they need to work, gave constructive feedback on loopholes of the website during meetings and made sure all topics needed for the website are covered during the meeting.

She faced financial challenges while growing up in a low-income background which prompted her to source scholarships that align with her mission and vision which is to also give back to the community and ALU came into the picture.

She is currently a second-year student at African Leadership University studying computing as a major, Funke is also a hairstylist and chef, she enjoys blogging, coding, and cooking.
