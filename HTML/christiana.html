  • Hi, I'm Christiana

    Christiana Oluwabunmi Ola is an aspiring Cyberspace Security Professional, an Entrepreneur and an Ambassador of the Apinke Girls Initiative. She is a Computing student at the African Leadership College, Mauritius. Christiana is a Nigerian by nationality and orginally from Oyo state. she resides with her family in Ijegun, Lagos state. She is the last child of a family of seven and the only daughter. Christiana is from the Yoruba tribe in Nigeria.

  • ALU Journey

    One day in late 2019, I was surfing the internet for scholarships that I could apply, I came across ALU as an advertisement and I became curious because I kept seeing advert every time as I continue searching for scholarships. I became interested and so I decided to check it out. I looked into the school online and saw that it was genuine. I also discovered that what the school stands for and its objectives match with my aims of becoming someone who can take control of his own path and effectively lead others. Then I made the decision to apply. I filled out my information, took the assessment, and passed, so i continued with the application.

    Surprisingly simple and clear was the application process. It was a step-by-step process. After passing the assessment, I started my main application, filled in all of my details, and wrote the required essays. After a month of waiting, I received an email congratulating me on my admittance to ALU. Then, I was offered the opportunity to apply for a scholarship. I was grateful for both the admittance and the scholarship, as well as the sponsors who made my journey to ALU possible.

    Moving to ALU was a wonderful and thrilling experience. I remember my first day of college being so beautiful, with flowers planted all around campus. Our seniors were quite warm and inviting to us. Other new students were nice as well, and everyone was eager to get to know one another and make friends.

    It was interesting to observe other students from other African nations. Orientation events and the Ikaze ceremony were quite helpful in getting to know one another better. We got to meet not just students from various countries, but also students from the same country, with whom we formed bonds. It was interesting to meet students from various countries, backgrounds, and cultural values. For instance, I witnessed firsthand how Rwandans are so united and get along so well because of their terrible genocide experience and now that they are of one tribe, making them one large family.

    Web Development, Database Development, Cyber Security, Mathematics, and Integrated Project are the courses I have been studying in Year 2. An object oriented database model for banking is an example of my work in Database Development; you may view it with this link.

    My advice to prospective students is to always take advantage of the opportunities that are available to you and to pursue and fulfill your goals and mission.

    During my stay at ALU, one of the most important activities I did was clean up a canal in Flacq, Mauritius, with a group of my fellow students and other students from other universities in September 2021, by removing plastic and bottle wastes to minimize water pollution and allow unfettered water flow. I joined the We Women ALC, and one of the benefits I received was the ImpactHER trainings, which have been incredibly enlightening and useful in terms of running a business. In addition, I was able to join the Huawei ICT Academy and I've taken courses in 5G technology and security.

  • Experience in Mauritius

    Mauritius is a very beautiful island and exploring the Island has been interesting and exciting. I haven’t been to a lot of tourist places in Mauritius but the places I have been to was memorable and fun.

    Mont Choisy Beach:
    My first visit to a beach ever was the Mont Choisy beach. The church I attend invited us to bond time on the beach. The beach was long, calm and beautiful. I got to know more of the indegenous people through this gathering. We enjoyed ourselves tremendously. We talked, ate, drank, danced and sang together. Here are some pictures i took of that day.

    Pamplemousses Garden:
    My friend and I went to the Pamplemousses garden close to campus to see the beautiful garden with large water lilies and flowers. It was a peaceful and lovely place for your family and friends to have a picnic and just chill. Here are some of the pictures we took.

    Odysseo Aquarium:
    I had heard about this new fantastic Aquarium that had recently opened in Port Louis from other students, so I wanted to go explore it. This aquarium was spectacular and beautiful. The beauty of the environment and the atmosphere it evoked awe in me. I saw a variety of sea creatures, including little fish, sharks, crayfish, jellyfish, and others.
